# General Conditions

This policy is issued in consideration of the premium charged and is
subject to all terms, conditions, and exclusions set forth herein. The
policy period begins at 12:01 a.m. on the inception date stated in the
declarations and ends at 12:01 a.m. on the expiration date so stated,
in each case at the address of the named insured.

Premium is due and payable in full on or before the inception date
unless an instalment plan is endorsed onto this policy. Late payment
shall not, of itself, void coverage; however, the insurer reserves the
right to cancel the policy for non-payment in accordance with the
notice provisions of this section.

The named insured may cancel this policy at any time by surrendering
it to the insurer or by mailing written notice stating when the
cancellation is to be effective. The insurer may cancel the policy by
mailing written notice to the named insured at the last address known
to the insurer, stating when not less than thirty days thereafter the
cancellation shall be effective. Where cancellation is for non-payment
of premium, ten days' notice is sufficient.

Mid-term changes to this policy must be requested in writing and become
effective only when endorsed by the insurer. Verbal representations,
whether by the insured, an agent, or a broker, do not vary the terms
of the policy.

The named insured shall give prompt notice of any change in the use,
occupancy, or ownership of the insured premises that materially
increases the risk. Failure to give such notice may, at the insurer's
discretion, void coverage for losses traceable to the unreported
change.

# Definitions

The following defined terms apply throughout this policy.

"Covered cause of loss" means a peril listed in the Named Perils
Covered section, subject to the exclusions and limitations set forth
elsewhere in the policy.

"Period of restoration" means the period beginning seventy-two hours
after the time of direct physical loss or damage caused by a covered
cause of loss and ending on the earlier of the date the property is
repaired, rebuilt, or replaced with reasonable speed and similar
quality, or the date business is resumed at a new permanent location.

"Actual cash value" means the cost to repair or replace the damaged
property with material of like kind and quality, less an allowance for
depreciation reflecting age, wear, and obsolescence at the time of
loss.

"Replacement cost" means the cost to repair or replace the damaged
property with new material of like kind and quality, without
deduction for depreciation, subject to the limit of insurance and the
coinsurance condition stated elsewhere in the policy.

"Named insured" means the entity or individual identified as such in
the declarations. Coverage extended to additional insureds, mortgagees,
or loss payees is governed by the relevant endorsement.

"Deductible" means the amount the named insured must bear before the
insurer's obligation to pay attaches, applied per occurrence unless
otherwise specified.

# Named Perils Covered

Subject to the exclusions and conditions of this policy, the insurer
will pay for direct physical loss of or damage to insured property
caused by any of the following covered causes of loss.

Fire. Hostile fire, including damage from heat, smoke, and the action
of firefighting, but excluding loss from a fire intentionally set by
the named insured or by a person acting at the named insured's
direction.

Lightning. Direct strike to insured property or to objects on the
insured premises, including damage from the resulting power surge to
electrical equipment present at the time of the strike.

Windstorm and hail. Loss caused by the direct action of wind or hail
on the insured property. Wind-driven rain, snow, sand, or dust is
covered only where wind first damages the building such that an
opening is created through which precipitation enters.

Explosion. Sudden and violent release of pressure or chemical reaction.
Excluded: explosion of steam boilers, steam pipes, steam turbines, or
steam engines owned by, leased by, or operated under the control of
the named insured (covered separately under a boiler and machinery
endorsement when one is attached).

Smoke. Sudden and accidental damage from smoke, other than smoke from
agricultural smudging or industrial operations.

Vandalism. Wilful and malicious damage to or destruction of the
insured property by a person other than the named insured, an officer
of the named insured, or a person acting at the named insured's
direction.

Sprinkler leakage. Leakage or discharge of any substance from an
automatic sprinkler system, including collapse of a tank that is part
of the system. Loss caused by freezing of the system is covered only
where the named insured used reasonable care to maintain heat in the
building.

Water damage from plumbing systems. Accidental discharge or leakage
from a plumbing, heating, or air-conditioning system, or from a
domestic appliance, that results in direct physical damage to the
insured property. Loss to the appliance or system itself is excluded;
the resulting water damage to other property is covered.

Civil commotion. Loss caused by riot, civil commotion, or strike,
including pillage and looting that occurs in the course of and at the
location of the disturbance, but excluding loss caused by war, warlike
action, insurrection, rebellion, or revolution.

# Exclusions

The insurer will not pay for loss or damage caused directly or
indirectly by any of the following, regardless of any other cause or
event that contributes concurrently or in any sequence to the loss.

Flood. Surface water, waves, tides, tidal waves, overflow of any body
of water, or spray therefrom, whether or not driven by wind. Coverage
for flood, where required, is provided only under a separate flood
endorsement, which is NOT attached to this policy.

Earthquake, volcanic eruption, landslide, mudslide, or other earth
movement. Coverage for earth movement, where required, is provided
only under a separate earth-movement endorsement, which is NOT
attached to this policy.

War, invasion, hostilities (whether war is declared or not), civil
war, rebellion, insurrection, military or usurped power, and any
weapon of war employing atomic fission, atomic fusion, or radioactive
material.

Terrorism, whether certified or non-certified, is excluded. Coverage
for certified acts of terrorism, where required, is provided only
under a Terrorism Risk Insurance Act endorsement, which is NOT
attached to this policy.

Wear and tear, gradual deterioration, rust, corrosion, latent defect,
inherent vice, or any quality in property that causes it to damage or
destroy itself.

Dishonest, fraudulent, or criminal acts by the named insured, an
officer of the named insured, or any person to whom the named insured
has entrusted the property, whether acting alone or in collusion with
others.

Mechanical breakdown, including rupture or bursting caused by
centrifugal force. Coverage for mechanical breakdown, where required,
is provided only under a separate equipment-breakdown endorsement.

Loss of market, loss of use, delay, or any other consequential loss,
except as expressly provided under the Business Interruption section
of this policy.

# Sub-Limits

The following sub-limits apply within, and not in addition to, the
overall limit of insurance. Each sub-limit is the maximum the insurer
will pay regardless of the number of items, locations, or occurrences.

Debris removal. The insurer will pay the reasonable cost to remove
debris of insured property caused by a covered cause of loss, up to
twenty-five percent of the amount paid for direct damage. Debris
removal is included within the limit of insurance and is not in
addition to it, except where the limit is exhausted; in that case, an
additional amount of up to ten thousand currency units is available
for debris removal only.

Ordinance or law. The insurer will pay the increased cost of repair,
rebuilding, or demolition required by the enforcement of any
ordinance or law regulating the construction, use, or repair of the
insured property, up to ten percent of the amount paid for direct
damage. The named insured must, in fact, repair or replace the
damaged property within twenty-four months of the loss for the
ordinance-or-law sub-limit to apply.

Valuable papers and records. The insurer will pay up to twenty-five
thousand currency units for direct physical loss of or damage to
valuable papers, records, drawings, manuscripts, or other documents,
including the cost of reconstructing the information they contain. Data
held only in electronic form is excluded under this sub-limit.

Pollutant cleanup. The insurer will pay up to ten thousand currency
units for the cost to extract pollutants from land or water at the
insured premises, where the discharge of the pollutants was caused by
a covered cause of loss and is reported within one hundred and eighty
days. The cost to test for or monitor pollutants in the absence of an
actual discharge is excluded.

# Business Interruption

Subject to the conditions of this policy, the insurer will pay the
actual loss of business income the named insured sustains, plus
necessary extra expense incurred, during the period of restoration
following direct physical loss of or damage to insured property
caused by a covered cause of loss.

A waiting period of seventy-two hours applies. The insurer is not
liable for any loss of business income sustained during the first
seventy-two hours of the period of restoration.

A coinsurance condition of eighty percent applies to the business
interruption limit. If, at the time of loss, the limit of insurance
for business interruption is less than eighty percent of the
twelve-month projected business income value, the named insured shall
bear that proportion of the loss.

Loss is valued at actual loss sustained, calculated as the business
income that would have been earned during the period of restoration
had no loss occurred, less expenses that do not necessarily continue
during the period of restoration. The named insured shall produce
records sufficient to substantiate the calculation.

Coverage ends on the earliest of the date the property is repaired,
rebuilt, or replaced with reasonable speed and similar quality, the
date business is resumed at a new permanent location, or the
expiration of the maximum period of indemnity stated in the
declarations.

# Duties After Loss

In the event of loss or damage to insured property, the named
insured shall, as a condition precedent to coverage:

Give prompt notice to the insurer or its authorised representative,
including a description of the property involved, the time and cause
of loss, and the extent of damage so far as known.

Take all reasonable steps to protect the property from further damage
and to preserve evidence of the loss. Reasonable expenses incurred in
this protection are included in the loss settlement.

Prepare an inventory of damaged personal property, distinguishing
between property damaged and property destroyed, with quantities,
descriptions, actual cash values, and amounts of loss claimed. The
inventory shall be supported by available receipts, invoices, and
records.

Permit the insurer to inspect the damaged property and to examine the
named insured's books and records as often as reasonably required.
Submit to examination under oath while not in the presence of any
other insured, and sign the transcript of such examination if
requested.

Cooperate with the insurer in all matters pertaining to the
adjustment of the loss, including the production of documents,
attendance at proceedings, and assistance in the prosecution of
subrogation rights.

No suit or action against the insurer for the recovery of any claim
under this policy shall be sustainable unless commenced within two
years from the date the cause of action accrues. The cause of action
is deemed to accrue on the date of the direct physical loss or damage,
not on any later date of the insurer's coverage decision.
